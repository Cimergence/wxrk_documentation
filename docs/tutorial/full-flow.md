# Full Candidate Flow Tutorial

This tutorial walks you through the complete WXRK flow from first login to a generated, export-ready CV and cover letter. Follow it in order.

**Estimated time:** 20–30 minutes

---

## Part 1 — Create your account and onboard

### 1.1 Sign up

1. Go to the WXRK app
2. Click **Sign up**
3. Use your email address or sign in with Google / GitHub

### 1.2 Complete onboarding

After signing in for the first time, the onboarding wizard launches automatically.

**Step 1 — Welcome**
Read the intro screen. Click **Get started**.

**Step 2 — Your Profile**

Fill in the following:

```
Full name:    Jane Doe
Location:     Berlin, Germany
LinkedIn URL: https://linkedin.com/in/janedoe
Target role:  Senior Product Manager
Career stage: Actively applying
```

Click **Continue**.

**Step 3 — Personality**

Option A — Take the mini test:

Answer all 8 questions honestly. The test takes about 2 minutes. Your four-letter MBTI type is computed automatically.

Option B — Know your type:

Click **I already know my type** and select your type from the grid (e.g. **ENFJ**).

**Step 4 — Ready**

Review your type and its associated tone profile. Click **Go to dashboard**.

---

## Part 2 — Build your Technical Review

This is the most important step. The richer your data, the better the output.

### 2.1 Open Technical Review

Click **Technical Review** in the left sidebar.

### 2.2 Add your most recent experience

Click **+ Add experience**. Fill in the details for your current or most recent role:

```
Company:      Meridian Analytics
Job title:    Senior Product Manager
Start date:   2022-03
End date:     [leave blank — toggle "Currently here"]
Location:     Berlin, Germany
Summary:      Led the 0-to-1 development of a real-time analytics dashboard
              used by 120+ enterprise clients. Coordinated across engineering,
              design, and sales to hit a 6-month go-to-market target.
```

Add skills:
- Technical: SQL, Figma, Amplitude, Jira
- General: Product strategy, Stakeholder management, Agile

### 2.3 Add a project

Click **+ Add project** within the experience:

```
Project name:  Real-time Analytics Dashboard
Impact metric: Reduced client time-to-insight from 4 hours to 15 minutes
Summary:       Defined requirements, ran discovery sprints with 8 enterprise
               clients, worked with a 5-engineer team to ship MVP in 14 weeks.
Tech stack:    React, PostgreSQL, Amplitude, Figma
```

### 2.4 Add a STAR story

Click **+ Add STAR story**:

```
Situation: The product roadmap had no clear prioritisation framework and
           engineers were pulled in different directions by three PMs.
Task:      I was asked to own the roadmap and restore focus.
Action:    I introduced a RICE scoring model, ran a 2-day workshop with
           stakeholders, and cut the backlog from 140 to 22 items.
Result:    Velocity increased by 35% in the next quarter and two key
           features shipped 3 weeks ahead of schedule.
Tags:      leadership, delivery under pressure, stakeholder
```

### 2.5 Add a second experience (recommended)

Repeat steps 2.2–2.4 for a previous role. More experiences = richer context for CV generation.

### 2.6 Save

Click **Save** (or use the keyboard shortcut `Ctrl/⌘ + S`). A version snapshot is created.

## Part 3 — Create a new application

### 3.1 Go to Applications

Click **Applications** in the left sidebar, then **+ New application**.

### 3.2 Add the job description

**URL mode (recommended):**

1. Copy the URL of a job posting (e.g. from LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever)
2. Paste it into the URL field
3. Click **Fetch** — wait 5–15 seconds for the page to be scraped
4. Review the extracted role and company name in the confirmation toast

**Paste mode:**

1. Switch to **Paste** tab
2. Copy the entire job description text
3. Paste into the text area

### 3.3 Add a personal context note (optional but recommended)

Expand **Personal context** and write why you want this role:

```
I'm excited about Meridian's move into predictive analytics. I've spent
the last 2 years building real-time dashboards and would love to apply
that to forecasting use cases. The size of the data team (12 engineers)
is exactly the scale where I've been most effective.
```

### 3.4 Create the application

Click **Create application**. You are redirected to the Application Detail page.

---

## Part 4 — Generate your documents

### 4.1 Check completeness

The application detail page shows a **Fit Analysis** tab. Open it to see how your profile matches the job description.

Look for:
- Overall match score
- Required skills you're missing — add them to your Technical Review if relevant
- Suggested improvements

### 4.2 Generate CV

Click **Generate CV** (or the dropdown arrow for more options).

- Status changes to `PENDING`
- A progress indicator appears
- Generation typically takes 10–30 seconds

### 4.3 Generate Cover Letter

Once the CV is done, click **Generate Cover Letter**.

WXRK will use:
- Your technical review experiences
- Your MBTI type (tone calibration)
- Your personal context note
- The job description

### 4.4 (Alternative) Generate both at once

Click the dropdown arrow next to **Generate CV** and select **Generate All** to run both in one request.

---

## Part 5 — Review and export

### 5.1 Review the CV

Open the **CV** tab:

- Read through the full output
- Click **Edit** to fix any wording you want to adjust
- Check that impact metrics are present and accurate

### 5.2 Review the Cover Letter

Open the **Cover Letter** tab:

- Check that the opening paragraph references the specific company and role
- Verify the tone feels like you (if not, try a different MBTI type and regenerate)
- Edit any section that feels generic

### 5.3 Export as PDF

Click **Export PDF** (available on both tabs). The PDF downloads to your device.

### 5.4 Update status

Once you've submitted the application externally, update the status in WXRK:

1. Click the status badge (shows **Generated**)
2. Select **Submitted** from the dropdown

---

## Part 6 — Track your application

### 6.1 Add a timeline note

In the **Timeline** section at the bottom of the application detail:

- Click **+ Note** to log when you submitted, who you contacted, etc.
- Add the recruiter or hiring manager name when you know it

### 6.2 Track progress

As the application progresses, update the status:

- **Interview** — when you receive an interview invite
- **Offer** — when an offer is made
- **Rejected** — if the application is closed

The Kanban view on the Applications page gives you a pipeline overview across all your active applications.
